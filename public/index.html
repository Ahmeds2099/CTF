What Is This Event?

A Fun Cybersecurity Puzzle Challenge

Think of it like an escape room — but digital. You'll receive a USB drive full of mysterious files (images, audio, text files, network logs). Your job is to figure out the hidden clues inside each file using free tools we'll teach you about.

Each clue you find is called a "flag" — submit it on our online portal to earn points. The more flags you find, the higher your rank. It's competitive, educational, and a lot of fun.

  • No coding or hacking experience needed
  • Open to ALL college students from any institution
  • Solo participation — just you and the evidence

How It Works

01

Register & Show Up

Pay ₹100, register on Luma, and arrive at T.John Institute on 28 April by 1:30 PM. Bring your laptop with the recommended tools already installed (see below).

02

Receive Your Evidence Kit

You'll get a USB drive with the breach evidence — images, logs, audio files, encrypted files, network dumps, and more. This is your investigation kit.

03

Analyze Files with Tools

Use steganography tools on images, network analyzers on PCAP files, hex editors on binaries, metadata extractors on photos, audio spectrum analyzers, and ciphers on encrypted text. Each tool reveals different clues.

04

Find Flags & Submit

Flags are hidden strings formatted like ACF{this_is_a_flag}. Submit each flag on our CTFd platform. Correct flags earn you points, and you'll climb the leaderboard.

05

Compete & Win

You have 2 hours. The top 3 players on the leaderboard win prizes. All participants receive certificates.
